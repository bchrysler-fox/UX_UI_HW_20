The Department of the Interior

The Department of the Interior is the section of the United States government responsible for the management of the common heritage of the United States, whether it is natural heritage or cultural heritage. However, the current design of the website does not communicate this in a clear way. It gives off the impression of an impersonal bureaucratic monolith. We sought to change this.

Aesthetic

The design of the website is very impersonal and intimidating, according to interviewed subjects. This made the propsect of locating information on the site stressful.

Communication

The website has very inconsistent communication regarding off-site links. Some links that lead off-site are labeled, but many are not. Additionally, Bureaus and Offices are grouped together when they are not really related, and they should be split apart.

Information

Information that someone interested in the Department of the Interior, or one of its Bureaus, is often difficult to find, with the information often requiring the search function to locate.

Problem Statement
In order to present an approachable website and department, the site needs to be simplified and modernized.
MidFi Prototype

The focus of the MidFi prototype was to heavily simplify the site and make the color scheme more approachable to the average user. Testers expressed an overwhelming preference for this prototype.

Style Guide

The style guide took inspiration from something natural, a picture of a leopard gecko. By forgoing the current design scheme and focusing on oranges and greens, the design scheme now celebrates the forests and the deserts and becomes more warming and welcoming to the average user.
